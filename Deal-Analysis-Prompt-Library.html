04Thin Market / Rural ARV Analysis
Rural and small-town markets have fewer comps. This prompt forces conservative ARV assumptions when data is thin.
⚡ Toby Potter AI Version

Rules for thin markets: 1. Extend comp search to 2-3 mile radius if needed 2. Go back up to 6 months for closed sales 3. Apply a 5-10% DISCOUNT to your ARV estimate due to thin data 4. Flag if fewer than 3 valid comps exist — that means insufficient data to make an offer 5. Note the average days on market for sold comps (DOM affects holding cost estimates) 6. Assess: is this market "cattle or people"? (Low density = long DOM = hard exit) Give me the ARV range with explicit confidence level and note how many valid comps you found.
🔄 Generic AI Version
I'm evaluating a property in a low-transaction-volume market where comparable sales are limited: Address: [FULL ADDRESS] Asking Price: $[PRICE] Details: [SQ FT] sqft, [BED/BATH], built [YEAR] Market: [Describe — rural, small town, etc.] Help me estimate the ARV with these constraints: - Widen the comp search radius to 2-3 miles - Accept comps up to 6 months old - Apply conservative assumptions since data is thin - Note how many valid comps you can find - If fewer than 3 solid comps exist, flag that ARV confidence is low - Estimate average days on market for this area Give me a conservative ARV range and your confidence level (High / Medium / Low / Insufficient Data).
In thin markets, be MORE conservative, not less. When you can't find 3-5 comparable closed sales, you don't have enough information to make an offer.

🔄 Generic AI Version
