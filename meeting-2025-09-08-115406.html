This coaching call, facilitated by Abby Falodun, connected real estate investment coach Siam Ibrahim with Toby Potter of The Unshakable Investor. PROGRAM FOCUS: RENTAL FOUNDATIONS & FLIP FLEXIBILITY

Siam's coaching program, established since January last year, primarily focuses on teaching students to acquire rental properties to build generational wealth. While fix-and-flips are also covered, the current market conditions have shifted the focus towards rentals due to deals "just sitting on the market" after renovation.

THE 60-DAY EXECUTION CATALYST

To combat student inaction, Siam's program offers a $2,000 gift card towards a down payment for a rental property, but this incentive expires in 60 days. This "implementation" strategy ensures students don't just consume information but actively make their first offer and acquire their first rental property.

UNLOCKING CAPITAL: THE PARTNERSHIP MODEL

Toby's program functions as a hard money lender with a private equity fund, providing capital and partnership for fix-and-flip deals nationwide. Toby will partner on the down payment and closing costs, fund 85% of the total cost with an interest-bearing loan, and then split profits 50/50, while the student acts as "boots on the ground" managing the project.

SCALING FOR EXPONENTIAL GROWTH

With Toby's capital and funding, Siam's students could do "significantly more" deals, scaling from potentially 25 deals per cohort to "10, 20 deals a year" per student. This partnership aims to replace a student's job income by building a consistent flow of profits from flips, ensuring a monthly check after about six months.

STRATEGIC MARKET SELECTION & PROPERTY TYPES

While Siam's students invest in markets like Atlanta, Philadelphia, Jersey, and Ohio, Toby prefers the "south southeast belt" (Texas, Tennessee, North Carolina, Georgia, Alabama) due to lower acquisition costs, where he can acquire "five deals for $300,000" versus million-dollar properties in high-cost areas like Seattle or South Florida. They also fund multi-unit properties and manufactured homes *if* they are on a concrete foundation, avoiding mobile homes or high-risk areas like Cook County, Chicago.
