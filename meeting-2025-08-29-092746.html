THE MULTIFAMILY ADVANTAGE

Francis demonstrates a strong understanding of profitable real estate, currently owning three duplexes in Waterville, Maine, which he fixes up and rents for approximately $2,000 per unit. His strategy nets him $20,000 to $24,000 per building annually, with a goal of acquiring ten such properties to achieve $200,000-$240,000 in net income, highlighting the stability of multi-family investments where one vacant unit doesn't jeopardize the entire investment.

STRATEGIC VALUE ADDITION

As his own general contractor, Francis meticulously oversees renovations, investing an additional $10,000-$15,000 per property for significant upgrades. Examples from his latest triplex at 2 School Street include replacing a heavy cast iron sink, installing new click flooring in high-traffic areas to withstand snowy boots, adding a crucial apartment door, and upgrading to energy-efficient LED lighting in the basement, all aimed at attracting high-quality tenants like the lawyer moving in downstairs.

VISIONARY DEAL SOURCING

Francis excels at identifying unconventional opportunities, such as the 10,000 square-foot former lawyer's office at 44-46 Elm Street, listed at $600,000 but for which he plans to offer $450,000. He envisions transforming this commercial property into two separate houses and a duplex by removing a breezeway, projecting an After Repair Value (ARV) of $850,000 by targeting executive-level tenants who would pay $3,500 per month. He wisely notes, "these are the best deals because nobody else can finance them."

UNLOCKING CAPITAL FOR GROWTH

Francis articulated his primary need for funding, stating, "If I had funding, I would have 10 or 20 properties within 12 months," indicating a clear pipeline of opportunities but a capital constraint. While he has $90,000 in the bank, he's prudent about deploying it all at once to avoid financial strain, requiring support for down payments to finance purchases and 100% of rehab costs.

TAILORED FUNDING SOLUTIONS

Recognizing Francis's extensive 38 years of experience and his role as his own general contractor, Toby Potter acknowledged, "I can't really coach you because you know as much as I do, if not more." Instead, Toby committed to being a "go to bank" that will "loan purchase price plus a hundred percent of the rehab," offering a nationwide draw system with quick fund wiring after work verification, and exploring partnership options for equity.
