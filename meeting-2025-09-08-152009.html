MEETING RECAP & FULL RECORDING

The coaching call focused on Chris's challenges with scaling his real estate investment business due to capital constraints. While Chris specializes profitably in buying and quickly flipping mobile homes, particularly utilizing Section 8 vouchers in LA County, Toby expressed his investment philosophy, which prioritizes value-add traditional real estate in other markets due to concerns about mobile home depreciation and regional risks like tornadoes. The conversation highlighted the different approaches to finding and financing deals.

ACCESS THE RECORDING

CAPITAL CONSTRAINTS LIMIT SCALE

Chris has identified a profitable niche in mobile home investing, often buying units for $10K-$141K and quickly reselling them for significant spreads, like one in escrow for $180K after a $15K purchase and $33K rehab. However, he and his partner are "tapped out" of cash, preventing them from acquiring more deals despite a rapid turnover model that can cycle money multiple times in a few months.

UNCONVENTIONAL NICHES YIELD CASH FLOW

Chris has found a successful strategy in mobile home investing, specifically in parks in LA County, acquiring properties ranging from $10,000 to $141,000. He highlighted a recent deal purchased for $15K, with $33K in rehab, now in escrow for $180K, demonstrating robust returns and the ability to leverage Section 8 housing vouchers for consistent income, despite the asset class typically depreciating.

VALUE-ADD VS. DEPRECIATION DILEMMA

Toby explicitly states he avoids mobile homes due to their depreciation and lack of value-add potential, citing regional risks like "tornado alley" in Texas. His preference is for single-family residences where he can acquire properties at "40, 50 cents on the dollar, put another 30 cents on the dollar and then sell it... at 100 cents on the dollar," aiming for appreciation and significant capital gains.

DEPRECIATING ASSET

VALUE-ADD PROPERTY

MASTERING PRECISION COMPING

Chris underscores the importance of precise comping, stating he wouldn't be interested if "there was no comps in the last 90 days within a half a quarter mile." He prefers tight filters, specifically a "quarter mile good 90 days," and even goes back "three years" in the same street area to validate value, ensuring a solid understanding of market potential before committing to a deal.

SPECIALIZATION SHAPES PARTNERSHIPS

Toby clearly defined his investment parameters, stating, "I don't do business in California, number one. And then I definitely don't do mobile homes." His refusal to partner on Chris's mobile home deals, despite their profitability, highlights how specific market focus and asset class preference are non-negotiable for forming investment partnerships, even offering to pass along mobile home leads to Chris.
