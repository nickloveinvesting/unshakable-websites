KEY CONCEPTS COVERED

CAPITAL VELOCITY THROUGH VALUE-ADD

Toby emphasized that for him to participate financially, a deal must be a "value-add" property, not a stabilized one. This means buying a property below market value, investing "construction money" or CAPEX for rehab (e.g., $200k-$300k on an $800k purchase), increasing its value, and then refinancing that construction loan within 12 months to get the initial capital back out. This "churn and burn" model is essential for freeing up capital for subsequent deals, unlike stabilized properties which often have 3-5 year prepay penalties preventing early refinance.

THE DEAL PIPELINE FACTORY

The coach stressed the "volume deal" aspect, encouraging Tate to analyze "10, 15 deals a day" and submit "two to three offers every day." This aggressive approach, exemplified by another student who got a contract in three weeks after reviewing 40 deals and submitting 17 for analysis, is crucial. The AI tool assists in rapidly vetting these deals, providing a "pursue or reject" verdict to streamline the process.

FORGING PARTNERSHIPS FOR GROWTH

For capital partners like Toby to participate, a 50% ownership in the deal entity (client's or coach's LLC) is required, as long as it's an equal partnership at closing. Toby stated, "I like putting it in yours because this gives you that experience, history and all that, and it makes it simpler for all of us," directly addressing Tate's concern about people wanting to see experience when negotiating.

STRATEGIC REHAB PHASES

Toby outlined a clear rehab hierarchy: always start with new flooring and paint ($3-5k "lipstick" cost). The next level includes kitchen (cabinets, countertops, appliances) and bathroom updates, costing $30-50k for a full cosmetic renovation. Beyond that, critical structural components like HVAC, electrical (especially aluminum wiring), and plumbing (PVC vs. cast iron) are assessed. This phased approach ensures maximum value increase for the investment.
