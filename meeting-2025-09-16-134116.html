RECYCLING CAPITAL FOR LONG-TERM WEALTH

Toby explained that "the fix and flip works so well to build you up" because it allows for a quick return on capital (161s). The process involves putting in a down payment/closing costs, doing construction, refinancing to get money back, splitting profit, and then rotating funds to the next deal (170s-177s). This strategy frees up capital to acquire buy-and-hold properties with the client's own money (178s-183s).

LOCAL PRESENCE IS POWER

Toby articulated his anxiety about investing in other cities: "I'm relying upon you to be my boots on the ground, to be my GC" (94s-101s). Without a local partner, "we're relying upon a contractor to blow smoke up our butts because I got nobody there to hold him accountable" (107s-113s). This underscores the critical importance of having reliable local oversight for successful rehabs and the limitations of remote investment.

PRE-MARKET POSITIONING

Toby advocated for an aggressive marketing strategy, advising to "immediately get it all over Zillow and all that, first and foremost to see what kind of bites" as a "coming soon" listing (741s-761s). This approach, starting the day ownership is taken and contractor work begins, aims to generate maximum traffic over 45 days, potentially securing offers even before completion, and crucially, avoiding the "60, $62,000" (772s) cost of a traditional realtor by exploring 4% or flat-fee services (792s).

HUNT FOR THE GOLDEN EGG
