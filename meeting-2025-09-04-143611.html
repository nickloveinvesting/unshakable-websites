KEY CONCEPTS COVERED

ACQUISITION IS KING

Toby emphasizes, "First things first, you make money on the purchase. If the math doesn't work on day one, you're just polishing a bad deal." For flips, the standard is 75% of After Repair Value (ARV). For rentals, the income approach requires positive cash flow after all costs, confirmed by actual rental comps, as exemplified by the Marysville deal where rent didn't cover the mortgage, leading to a "hard no" (719.13-721.65, 762.82-772.28).

THE VELOCITY OF DEALS

Toby stresses, "When we see a deal, we crunch a deal, we run the numbers... submit an offer" (1326.08-1329.0). He cited a peer who lost an 84-unit complex by analyzing for a week instead of acting (1957.18-1963.98). In hot markets like Seattle, being a "machine" that processes 3-5 deals and submits 2-3 offers daily, even low-ball, creates opportunities (1100.66-1104.1, 1337.6-1343.74).

THE FUNNEL OF OPPORTUNITY

Toby outlines building a "huge funnel" (1150.09) by submitting 2-3 low-ball offers daily across 3 chosen neighborhoods (1164.77-1170.29). While 90% may be rejected, this strategy maintains "rapport" with listing agents (1186.38-1190.46), leading them to contact the buyer when sellers become desperate, as demonstrated by the 94-unit apartment complex acquired for $5,000 after 90 days of waiting (1203.92-1236.73).

PARTNERED CAPITAL ACCELERATION

Ankit's "long term plan is to buy and hold and start making passive income" (417.74-421.5), and he sees Toby as his "mentor" for funding (1580.69-1588.24). Toby's company, Direct Funding Resource, offers hard money loans for 85-90% of deals, and Toby will cover the remaining 10-15% equity gap, sharing 50/50 profits without charging interest on his capital (1783.91-1883.92, 1829.62-1835.19).

LOGIC-DRIVEN ACQUISITIONS

Toby instructs, "We wear them down with the facts" (2081.28-2083.64). Agents should "tell the seller why we can only pay 450. Because we got to put 60, $70,000 in rehab. It's only worth X amount of dollars" (2065.09-2073.09). This approach, using clear math and market realities, allows for aggressive offers by demonstrating the "numbers make sense" (2078.64-2079.76), ensuring a profitable acquisition rather than buying a "headache" (770.08-772.28).

OPTIMIZED WEALTH CYCLE

Toby outlines a clear 3-5 year cycle for multifamily properties: "Year one we buy and stabilize. Year two and three, we collect rents. Year four and five we sell" (1462.05-1469.01). This dynamic approach means identifying market lows for acquisition (e.g., when the economy is "in the toilet" and sellers are "desperate" (1472.85-1481.73)) and selling at market highs (e.g., when interest rates drop, increasing perceived value for buyers (1483.1-1498.14)), continuously reinvesting profits for exponential growth.
