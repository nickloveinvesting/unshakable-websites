FORMALIZING A FAMILY REAL ESTATE LEGACY

Kane Sherman, an experienced senior housing and multifamily developer, aims to establish a real estate company for his sons to manage, focusing on acquiring fourplexes and larger properties for long-term residuals. His objective is to create a "good formalized plan" that shows them how to succeed with "as little of my cash as possible putting on the table."

UNLOCKING VALUE-ADD OPPORTUNITIES

Toby showcased his firm's ability to identify deeply undervalued assets, such as a 109-unit senior living facility bought for $750,000 and transformed with $4 million into a $10 million apartment complex. Another example involved a 94-unit property in Longview, only "60% occupied, 20% collection" due to "squalor," which was acquired, funded with an additional $1 million line of credit, and converted into a highly profitable asset currently valued at "six and a half million" from a "3.1 million" assumption.

STRATEGIC FINANCING & PARTNERSHIP

Global Integrity Finance offers robust financing options, specifically providing "interest-only until they're stabilized" for construction loans, which are then refinanced into permanent financing. For larger capital needs, they also partner with investors, securing the "capital and the down payment, close the cost" while the client contributes "sweat equity" as the project manager, as discussed for a potential $250,000 capital need.

LEVERAGING MARKET CYCLES

Toby emphasized his foresight into market cycles, anticipating "interest rates are getting ready to drop next month" and "three more times after that one," which will lead to a "real estate boom." This strategic timing allows his team to "acquire as much property as we can nationwide" ahead of the market surge, as demonstrated by their acquisition of almost "300 doors" with a "$7.5 million net profit" during a perceived market downturn.

MENTORSHIP FOR NEXT-GENERATION INVESTORS

Toby acknowledged Kane's extensive experience, stating he was "overqualified" for the coaching program, but suggested it would be a "great candidate" for Kane's sons, who lack formal education but desire to enter real estate. The program provides 30 years of knowledge, "tools, resources and capital to ensure that these deals happen correctly and profitably," specifically designed to prevent new entrants from making mistakes or "los[ing] his ass."
